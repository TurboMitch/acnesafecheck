What is Lanolin Alcohol?

Lanolin Alcohol is an occlusive that forms a barrier to lock in moisture. You'll most often see it in barrier balms, ointments and heavy night creams.

Does Lanolin Alcohol clog pores?

Rarely — Lanolin Alcohol has only a mild pore-clogging risk (rated 2/5) and suits most skin types, though very acne-prone skin may want to patch test. How we rate comedogenicity — and its limits

The 0–5 comedogenicity scale comes from dermatology research that measured how much individual ingredients clogged pores, originally using the rabbit-ear assay (Fulton et al., 1984). A rating reflects an ingredient's potential to clog pores at high concentration — not a guarantee that any finished product will break you out.

That distinction matters. A later review (Draelos & DiNardo, 2006) found that a single ingredient's comedogenic score does not reliably predict how a complete formula behaves on real skin: concentration, the rest of the formula, and your own skin all change the result. So read Lanolin Alcohol's 2/5 as a risk signal — most useful when the ingredient sits near the top of the list — rather than a verdict on one specific product.
