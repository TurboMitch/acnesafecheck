Is Evening Primrose Oil comedogenic?

Quick answer: Evening Primrose Oil has a comedogenicity rating of 2/5 — a mild, low pore-clogging risk. It is also a common fungal-acne (Malassezia) trigger.

2/5
A mild, low pore-clogging risk
Comedogenicity scale: 0 = won't clog pores, 5 = very likely to clog pores.

Evening Primrose Oil carries only a mild pore-clogging risk. It is fine for most skin types; very reactive or acne-prone skin may still want to patch test a new product that contains it.

What is Evening Primrose Oil?

Evening Primrose Oil is a plant or carrier oil used to moisturise and condition skin. On an ingredient label it may also appear as oenothera biennis oil. You'll most often see it in facial oils, cleansing oils, balms, lip products and richer moisturisers.

Fungal-acne (Malassezia) safe? No — this is a common Malassezia (fungal acne) trigger, so avoid it if you have fungal acne. Check a whole product for fungal-acne triggers →

Does Evening Primrose Oil clog pores?

Rarely — Evening Primrose Oil has only a mild pore-clogging risk (rated 2/5) and suits most skin types, though very acne-prone skin may want to patch test. How to read this rating

A comedogenicity rating is general guidance compiled from published reference lists, not a lab result for a specific formula. An ingredient near the end of an ingredient list is present in a small amount, so its rating matters less than one near the top. Read our full sources & methodology.
