Is Linseed / Flaxseed Oil comedogenic?

Quick answer: Linseed / Flaxseed Oil has a comedogenicity rating of 4/5 — high pore-clogging risk. It is also a common fungal-acne (Malassezia) trigger.

4/5
High pore-clogging risk
Comedogenicity scale: 0 = won't clog pores, 5 = very likely to clog pores.

Linseed / Flaxseed Oil sits high on the comedogenic scale. For acne-prone or breakout-prone skin it is one of the ingredients most likely to block pores and contribute to blackheads, whiteheads and pimples, especially when it appears near the top of an ingredient list (higher concentration).

What is Linseed / Flaxseed Oil?

Linseed / Flaxseed Oil is a plant or carrier oil used to moisturise and condition skin. On an ingredient label it may also appear as flaxseed oil, linseed oil, linum usitatissimum seed oil. You'll most often see it in facial oils, cleansing oils, balms, lip products and richer moisturisers.

Fungal-acne (Malassezia) safe? No — this is a common Malassezia (fungal acne) trigger, so avoid it if you have fungal acne. Check a whole product for fungal-acne triggers →

Does Linseed / Flaxseed Oil clog pores?

Yes — Linseed / Flaxseed Oil is one of the higher pore-clogging ingredients (rated 4/5) and is best avoided in leave-on products if you are acne-prone. Remember that where an ingredient sits in the list matters: Linseed / Flaxseed Oil near the top means a higher concentration and more impact than the same ingredient near the end.

Acne-safe alternatives to Linseed / Flaxseed Oil

If Linseed / Flaxseed Oil breaks you out, these lower-rated oil options are far less likely to clog pores:

  • Sunflower Oil — Acne safe · 0/5
  • Camellia / Tsubaki Oil — Low risk · 1/5
  • Castor Oil — Low risk · 1/5
  • Grapeseed Oil — Low risk · 1/5
  • Hydrogenated Castor Oil — Low risk · 1/5

How to read this rating

A comedogenicity rating is general guidance compiled from published reference lists, not a lab result for a specific formula. An ingredient near the end of an ingredient list is present in a small amount, so its rating matters less than one near the top. Read our full sources & methodology.
