Is Beeswax comedogenic?

Quick answer: Beeswax has a comedogenicity rating of 2/5 — a mild, low pore-clogging risk. It is not a common fungal-acne trigger.

2/5
A mild, low pore-clogging risk
Comedogenicity scale: 0 = won't clog pores, 5 = very likely to clog pores.

Beeswax carries only a mild pore-clogging risk. It is fine for most skin types; very reactive or acne-prone skin may still want to patch test a new product that contains it.

What is Beeswax?

Beeswax is a wax used to give structure to balms and sticks. On an ingredient label it may also appear as cera alba. You'll most often see it in balms, sticks, mascaras and solid formats.

Fungal-acne (Malassezia) safe? Yes — it is not on common Malassezia (fungal acne) trigger lists. Check a whole product for fungal-acne triggers →

Does Beeswax clog pores?

Rarely — Beeswax has only a mild pore-clogging risk (rated 2/5) and suits most skin types, though very acne-prone skin may want to patch test. Remember that where an ingredient sits in the list matters: Beeswax near the top means a higher concentration and more impact than the same ingredient near the end.

How to read this rating

A comedogenicity rating is general guidance compiled from published reference lists, not a lab result for a specific formula. An ingredient near the end of an ingredient list is present in a small amount, so its rating matters less than one near the top. Read our full sources & methodology.
