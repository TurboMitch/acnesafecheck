Is Hydrogenated Lecithin comedogenic?

Hydrogenated Lecithin is an emulsifier that keeps oil and water mixed in a formula. Its comedogenicity rating is 1/5 — a very low pore-clogging risk.

1/5
A very low pore-clogging risk
Comedogenicity scale: 0 = won't clog pores, 5 = very likely to clog pores.

Hydrogenated Lecithin has a very low likelihood of clogging pores and is generally considered safe for acne-prone skin.

What is Hydrogenated Lecithin?

Hydrogenated Lecithin is an emulsifier that keeps oil and water mixed in a formula. On a product label it may also appear under names such as: hydrogenated lecithin.
