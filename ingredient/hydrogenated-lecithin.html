Is Hydrogenated Lecithin comedogenic?

Quick answer: Hydrogenated Lecithin has a comedogenicity rating of 1/5 — a very low pore-clogging risk. It is also a common fungal-acne (Malassezia) trigger.

1/5

What is Hydrogenated Lecithin?

Hydrogenated Lecithin is an emulsifier that keeps oil and water mixed in a formula. You'll most often see it in creams, lotions and any product that blends oil and water.

Fungal-acne (Malassezia) safe? No — this is a common Malassezia (fungal acne) trigger, so avoid it if you have fungal acne. Check a whole product for fungal-acne triggers →

Does Hydrogenated Lecithin clog pores?

Unlikely — Hydrogenated Lecithin has a very low pore-clogging risk (rated 1/5) and is generally fine for acne-prone skin.
