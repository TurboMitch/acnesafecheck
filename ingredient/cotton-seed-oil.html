Is Cotton Seed Oil comedogenic?

Quick answer: Cotton Seed Oil has a comedogenicity rating of 3/5 — moderate pore-clogging risk. It is also a common fungal-acne (Malassezia) trigger.

3/5
Moderate pore-clogging risk
Comedogenicity scale: 0 = won't clog pores, 5 = very likely to clog pores.

Cotton Seed Oil has a moderate chance of clogging pores. Plenty of people tolerate it, but if you break out easily it is worth watching — particularly if it is one of the first few ingredients listed.

What is Cotton Seed Oil?

Cotton Seed Oil is a plant or carrier oil used to moisturise and condition skin. On an ingredient label it may also appear as cottonseed oil, gossypium oil. You'll most often see it in facial oils, cleansing oils, balms, lip products and richer moisturisers.

Fungal-acne (Malassezia) safe? No — this is a common Malassezia (fungal acne) trigger, so avoid it if you have fungal acne. Check a whole product for fungal-acne triggers →

Does Cotton Seed Oil clog pores?

It can — Cotton Seed Oil carries a moderate pore-clogging risk (rated 3/5), so watch it if you break out easily, especially when it is high on the ingredient list. Remember that where an ingredient sits in the list matters: Cotton Seed Oil near the top means a higher concentration and more impact than the same ingredient near the end.

How to read this rating

A comedogenicity rating is general guidance compiled from published reference lists, not a lab result for a specific formula. An ingredient near the end of an ingredient list is present in a small amount, so its rating matters less than one near the top. Read our full sources & methodology.
