What is Grapeseed Oil?

Grapeseed Oil is a plant or carrier oil used to moisturise and condition skin. On an ingredient label it may also appear as grape seed oil, vitis vinifera seed oil. You'll most often see it in facial oils, cleansing oils, balms, lip products and richer moisturisers.

How we rate comedogenicity — and its limits

The 0–5 comedogenicity scale comes from dermatology research that measured how much individual ingredients clogged pores, originally using the rabbit-ear assay (Fulton et al., 1984). A rating reflects an ingredient's potential to clog pores at high concentration — not a guarantee that any finished product will break you out.

That distinction matters. A later review (Draelos & DiNardo, 2006) found that a single ingredient's comedogenic score does not reliably predict how a complete formula behaves on real skin: concentration, the rest of the formula, and your own skin all change the result. So read Grapeseed Oil's 1/5 as a risk signal — most useful when the ingredient sits near the top of the list — rather than a verdict on one specific product.
