Is Octyl Palmitate comedogenic?

Quick answer: Octyl Palmitate has a comedogenicity rating of 4/5 — high pore-clogging risk. It is also a common fungal-acne (Malassezia) trigger.

4/5
High pore-clogging risk
Comedogenicity scale: 0 = won't clog pores, 5 = very likely to clog pores.

Octyl Palmitate sits high on the comedogenic scale. For acne-prone or breakout-prone skin it is one of the ingredients most likely to block pores and contribute to blackheads, whiteheads and pimples, especially when it appears near the top of an ingredient list (higher concentration).

What is Octyl Palmitate?

Octyl Palmitate is an emollient ester that gives products a smooth, silky feel. On an ingredient label it may also appear as ethylhexyl palmitate. You'll most often see it in foundations, primers, sunscreens, lotions and anything with a light, silky slip.

Fungal-acne (Malassezia) safe? No — this is a common Malassezia (fungal acne) trigger, so avoid it if you have fungal acne. Check a whole product for fungal-acne triggers →

Does Octyl Palmitate clog pores?

Yes — Octyl Palmitate is one of the higher pore-clogging ingredients (rated 4/5) and is best avoided in leave-on products if you are acne-prone. Remember that where an ingredient sits in the list matters: Octyl Palmitate near the top means a higher concentration and more impact than the same ingredient near the end.

Acne-safe alternatives to Octyl Palmitate

If Octyl Palmitate breaks you out, these lower-rated ester options are far less likely to clog pores:

  • Caprylic/Capric Triglyceride — Low risk · 1/5
  • Cetearyl Ethylhexanoate — Low risk · 1/5
  • Coco-Caprylate — Low risk · 1/5
  • Diisopropyl Adipate — Low risk · 1/5
  • Glyceryl Stearate — Low risk · 1/5

How to read this rating

A comedogenicity rating is general guidance compiled from published reference lists, not a lab result for a specific formula. An ingredient near the end of an ingredient list is present in a small amount, so its rating matters less than one near the top. Read our full sources & methodology.
