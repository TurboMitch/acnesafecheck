Is Laureth-23 comedogenic?

Quick answer: Laureth-23 has a comedogenicity rating of 3/5 — moderate pore-clogging risk. It is also a common fungal-acne (Malassezia) trigger.

3/5
Moderate pore-clogging risk
Comedogenicity scale: 0 = won't clog pores, 5 = very likely to clog pores.

Laureth-23 has a moderate chance of clogging pores. Plenty of people tolerate it, but if you break out easily it is worth watching — particularly if it is one of the first few ingredients listed.

What is Laureth-23?

Laureth-23 is an emulsifier that keeps oil and water mixed in a formula. You'll most often see it in creams, lotions and any product that blends oil and water.

Fungal-acne (Malassezia) safe? No — this is a common Malassezia (fungal acne) trigger, so avoid it if you have fungal acne. Check a whole product for fungal-acne triggers →

Does Laureth-23 clog pores?

It can — Laureth-23 carries a moderate pore-clogging risk (rated 3/5), so watch it if you break out easily, especially when it is high on the ingredient list. Remember that where an ingredient sits in the list matters: Laureth-23 near the top means a higher concentration and more impact than the same ingredient near the end.

How to read this rating

A comedogenicity rating is general guidance compiled from published reference lists, not a lab result for a specific formula. An ingredient near the end of an ingredient list is present in a small amount, so its rating matters less than one near the top. Read our full sources & methodology.
