Is Apricot Kernel Oil comedogenic?

Quick answer: Apricot Kernel Oil has a comedogenicity rating of 2/5 — a mild, low pore-clogging risk. It is also a common fungal-acne (Malassezia) trigger.

What is Apricot Kernel Oil?

Apricot Kernel Oil is a plant or carrier oil used to moisturise and condition skin. On an ingredient label it may also appear as prunus armeniaca kernel oil. You'll most often see it in facial oils, cleansing oils, balms, lip products and richer moisturisers.

Fungal-acne (Malassezia) safe? No — this is a common Malassezia (fungal acne) trigger, so avoid it if you have fungal acne. Check a whole product for fungal-acne triggers →

Rarely — Apricot Kernel Oil has only a mild pore-clogging risk (rated 2/5) and suits most skin types, though very acne-prone skin may want to patch test. How we rate comedogenicity — and its limits

The 0–5 comedogenicity scale comes from dermatology research that measured how much individual ingredients clogged pores, originally using the rabbit-ear assay (Fulton et al., 1984). A rating reflects an ingredient's potential to clog pores at high concentration — not a guarantee that any finished product will break you out.

That distinction matters. A later review (Draelos & DiNardo, 2006) found that a single ingredient's comedogenic score does not reliably predict how a complete formula behaves on real skin: concentration, the rest of the formula, and your own skin all change the result. So read Apricot Kernel Oil's 2/5 as a risk signal — most useful when the ingredient sits near the top of the list — rather than a verdict on one specific product.
