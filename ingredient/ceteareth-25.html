Is Ceteareth-25 comedogenic?

Quick answer: Ceteareth-25 has a comedogenicity rating of 2/5 — a mild, low pore-clogging risk. It is also a common fungal-acne (Malassezia) trigger.

2/5
A mild, low pore-clogging risk
Comedogenicity scale: 0 = won't clog pores, 5 = very likely to clog pores.

Ceteareth-25 carries only a mild pore-clogging risk. It is fine for most skin types; very reactive or acne-prone skin may still want to patch test a new product that contains it.

What is Ceteareth-25?

Ceteareth-25 is an emulsifier that keeps oil and water mixed in a formula. You'll most often see it in creams, lotions and any product that blends oil and water.

Fungal-acne (Malassezia) safe? No — this is a common Malassezia (fungal acne) trigger, so avoid it if you have fungal acne. Check a whole product for fungal-acne triggers →

Does Ceteareth-25 clog pores?

Rarely — Ceteareth-25 has only a mild pore-clogging risk (rated 2/5) and suits most skin types, though very acne-prone skin may want to patch test. Remember that where an ingredient sits in the list matters: Ceteareth-25 near the top means a higher concentration and more impact than the same ingredient near the end.

How to read this rating
