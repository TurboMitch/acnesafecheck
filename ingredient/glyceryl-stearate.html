Is Glyceryl Stearate comedogenic?

Quick answer: Glyceryl Stearate has a comedogenicity rating of 1/5 — a very low pore-clogging risk. It is also a common fungal-acne (Malassezia) trigger.

What is Glyceryl Stearate?

Glyceryl Stearate is an emollient ester that gives products a smooth, silky feel. You'll most often see it in foundations, primers, sunscreens, lotions and anything with a light, silky slip.

Fungal-acne (Malassezia) safe? No — this is a common Malassezia (fungal acne) trigger, so avoid it if you have fungal acne. Check a whole product for fungal-acne triggers →

Does Glyceryl Stearate clog pores?

Unlikely — Glyceryl Stearate has a very low pore-clogging risk (rated 1/5) and is generally fine for acne-prone skin. Remember that where an ingredient sits in the list matters: Glyceryl Stearate near the top means a higher concentration and more impact than the same ingredient near the end.
