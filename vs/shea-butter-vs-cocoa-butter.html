The verdict

Shea Butter is the safer choice for acne-prone skin: it rates 0/5 (non-comedogenic (acne safe)) versus 4/5 for Cocoa Butter (a high pore-clogging risk). One caveat: Shea Butter can still feed Malassezia, so it isn't the best pick if fungal acne is your problem.

Shea Butter: 0/5

Shea Butter rates 0/5 — non-comedogenic (acne safe). It is not known to clog pores and is generally a safe choice for acne-prone skin. It can also feed Malassezia, so people with fungal acne usually avoid it. Full breakdown: is Shea Butter comedogenic?

Cocoa Butter: 4/5

Cocoa Butter rates 4/5 — a high pore-clogging risk. If you break out easily, avoid it in leave-on products, especially when it appears near the top of the ingredient list. It can also feed Malassezia, so people with fungal acne usually avoid it. Full breakdown: is Cocoa Butter comedogenic?
