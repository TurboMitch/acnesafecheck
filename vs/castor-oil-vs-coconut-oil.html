Castor Oil vs Coconut Oil: which is better for acne-prone skin?

Quick answer: Castor Oil is the safer pick for acne-prone skin. Castor Oil rates 1/5 and Coconut Oil rates 4/5 on the comedogenic scale.

The verdict

Castor Oil is the safer choice for acne-prone skin: it rates 1/5 (a very low pore-clogging risk) versus 4/5 for Coconut Oil (a high pore-clogging risk). One caveat: Castor Oil can still feed Malassezia, so it isn't the best pick if fungal acne is your problem.

Castor Oil: 1/5

Castor Oil rates 1/5 — a very low pore-clogging risk. Most skin tolerates it well; very reactive skin may want to patch test. It can also feed Malassezia, so people with fungal acne usually avoid it. Full breakdown: is Castor Oil comedogenic?

Coconut Oil: 4/5

Coconut Oil rates 4/5 — a high pore-clogging risk. If you break out easily, avoid it in leave-on products, especially when it appears near the top of the ingredient list. It can also feed Malassezia, so people with fungal acne usually avoid it. Full breakdown: is Coconut Oil comedogenic?
