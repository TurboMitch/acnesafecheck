Coconut Oil vs Jojoba Oil: which is better for acne-prone skin?

Quick answer: Jojoba Oil is the safer pick for acne-prone skin. Coconut Oil rates 4/5 and Jojoba Oil rates 2/5 on the comedogenic scale.

The verdict

Jojoba Oil is the safer choice for acne-prone skin: it rates 2/5 (a mild, low pore-clogging risk) versus 4/5 for Coconut Oil (a high pore-clogging risk). One caveat: Jojoba Oil can still feed Malassezia, so it isn't the best pick if fungal acne is your problem.

Coconut Oil: 4/5

Coconut Oil rates 4/5 — a high pore-clogging risk. If you break out easily, avoid it in leave-on products, especially when it appears near the top of the ingredient list. It can also feed Malassezia, so people with fungal acne usually avoid it. Full breakdown: is Coconut Oil comedogenic?

Jojoba Oil: 2/5

Jojoba Oil rates 2/5 — a mild, low pore-clogging risk. Most skin tolerates it well; very reactive skin may want to patch test. It can also feed Malassezia, so people with fungal acne usually avoid it. Full breakdown: is Jojoba Oil comedogenic?
